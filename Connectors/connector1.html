Connector Option 1

Wago Connection

The Wago style design stems from personal experience using the “Wago” brand connectors in residential and commercial electrical systems. The Wago connector is essentially a lever operated device that terminates wires together just like a “wire-nut” would do. The connector works by clamping a wire to a centrally located miniature bus bar located inside the insulated device. The lever closes and the wire is pinned to the bottom plate. These connectors come in several different sizes, ranging from 2 wires to upwards of 10 wires. Our team has in mind to create a connector that uses this lever operated system on a bus bar that can be mounted to a central grounding or power point. This connector will essentially remove the process of torquing a terminal screw to a certain poundage. The user will simply strip the insulation off the wire and slide the bare copper into the desired slot and close the lever. The connector does not damage the wire, it simply clamps it down just as a terminal screw would do. The only foreseeable problem with this design is making it so that it has the proper, consistent pressure on the wire. The product will need to undergo rigorous testing to determine its durability when exposed to extreme vibration. The connector will be created with the proper materials (stainless steel or aluminum components) to withstand exposure to moisture, hydraulic fluid, and extreme air flow.
